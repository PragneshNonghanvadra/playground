# Interview Notes: <Topic>

## 30-second explanation

The shortest honest version. Aim for "I could explain this in an elevator." No jargon you can't define.

## 2-minute explanation

A clear, structured explanation. Define terms. Give one concrete example. Mention one tradeoff.

## Deep-dive explanation

The full mental model: history, tradeoffs, alternatives, failure modes, scaling implications. What you'd write on a whiteboard if asked to teach this.

## Common interview questions

Questions an interviewer might ask. Bullet list.

## Strong answer patterns

What strong answers tend to include: structure, tradeoffs named, failure modes mentioned, scale considered, real-world examples.

## Weak answer patterns

What weak answers look like: only naming tools, no tradeoffs, ignoring failure modes, no concrete numbers, hand-waving on scale.

Sketches/architectures to be able to draw quickly.

## Tradeoffs to mention

## Production concerns to mention

The production gotchas that signal experience (e.g., partial failure, retries, idempotency, observability, rollback, multi-tenant safety).
